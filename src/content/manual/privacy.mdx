This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use these services.

## Information We Collect

### Information you provide directly

- Contact form submissions, including your name, email address, and message content.
- NukeTalk forum posts, replies, direct messages, and profile information.
- NukeLab server names, workspace preferences, and shared-volume content you create.
- NRMS search queries, filters, and saved visualizations.
- NukeAnalytics SQL Lab queries, charts, dashboards, and saved datasets.
- Communications with us via email or support channels.

### Information from NukeAuth

When you sign in or create an account through NukeAuth, we collect:

- Your name, username, email address, and profile avatar.
- The authentication provider you used (email/password, GitHub, Google, Microsoft, or LinkedIn).
- Role assignments, permissions, and group memberships.
- Session and security tokens required to keep you signed in.

### Information from NukeLab

When you use NukeLab, we process:

- Server lifecycle events, resource usage metrics, and credit transactions.
- Container logs, audit logs, and IP addresses for security, debugging, and queue management.
- Persistent workspace data, files, and shared volumes you create or upload.

### Information from NRMS

When you use NRMS, we process:

- Search queries, filters, and reactor selections used to explore the PRIS database.
- Saved visualizations, export requests, and account-linked preferences.
- IP addresses and access logs for security and usage analytics.

### Information from NukeAnalytics

When you use NukeAnalytics, we process:

- SQL Lab queries, chart and dashboard definitions, and dataset connections.
- Query execution logs, error logs, and usage metrics.
- Saved reports and collaborative workspace content you create or share.

### Automatically collected information

- Your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, visited pages, time spent, and referral URLs.
- Device and network information collected by Cloudflare and our server logs.
- Cookies, local storage, and authentication tokens used for security and preferences.

## How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

- Provide, operate, and maintain the Site, NukeLab, NukeTalk, NukeAuth, NRMS, and NukeAnalytics.
- Authenticate users, enforce role-based access controls, and keep accounts secure.
- Manage NukeLab resource plans, billing credits, server queues, and usage limits.
- Process NRMS search and visualization requests and NukeAnalytics queries, charts, and dashboards.
- Communicate with you via NukeMail about account activity, security alerts, and support requests.
- Improve our services, diagnose issues, and monitor usage patterns.
- Detect and prevent abuse, fraud, unauthorized access, and other security incidents.

## Cookies And Similar Technologies

We use cookies and local storage for authentication, security, and analytics. You can disable cookies in your browser, but some features — including sign-in — may not function correctly.

## Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described above, or as required by law:

- **Contact form messages:** retained for support and quality purposes, then deleted.
- **NukeLab server metrics and audit logs:** retained for operational and security review, then aggregated or deleted to optimize server storage.
- **NukeTalk content:** retained while your account is active; individual posts or accounts can be deleted by you or by admins.
- **NRMS saved visualizations and preferences:** retained while your account is active; deleted when your account is removed.
- **NukeAnalytics queries, charts, and dashboards:** retained while your account is active; deleted when your account is removed.
- **NukeAuth account data:** retained until you request account deletion.

## Security

We use commercially reasonable measures to protect your information, including encryption in transit, hardened containers, access controls, audit logging, and rate limiting. No method of transmission or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
